Hayao Miyazaki

Time Line of Miyasaki's life and work:

  • 1941 - Born in Bunkyō, Tokyo.
  • 1963 - Joined Toei Animation as artist. On Toei, Miyasaki contributed in many works, such as Doggie March and Animal Treasure Island.
  • 1971 - Moved to A-Pro where he co-directed Lupin the Third Part I.
  • 1979 - He moved to Tokyo Movie Shinsha where he directed The Castle of Cagliostro.
  • 1984 - He directed Nausicaä of the Valley of the Wind, produced by Topcraft.
  • 1985 - Miyasaki co-founded the Studios Ghibli.
  • 1986 - He directed Castle in the Sky
  • 1988 - He directed My Neighbor Totoro
  • 1989 - He directed Kiki’s Delivery Service
  • 1992 - He directed Porco Rosso
  • 1997 - He directed Princess Mononoke, gaining huge notority in the west. It was the first animated movie to ever win the Japan Academy Prize for Picture of the Year.
  • 2001 - He directed Spirited Away, the highest-grossing film in Japan by that time. The movie also won the Academy Award for Best Animated Feature.
  • 2004 - He directed Howl's Moving Castle
  • 2008 - He directed Ponyo
  • 2013 - He directed The Wind Rises. Miyazaki was also awarded the Academy Honorary Award at the Governors Awards
  • 2016 - Miyazaki proposed a new feature-length film, How Do You Live?, on which he began animation work without receiving official approval.
  • Present - Miyazaki still working on How Do You Live?, a friend of the director gave an interview to a japanese TV program explaining that Miyasaki wants to finish the project as a gift for his grandson.
